About

I am a PhD candidate in Combinatorics & Optimization at the University of Waterloo, advised by Chaitanya Swamy. My research focuses on approximation algorithms, combinatorial optimization, stochastic optimization, and graph algorithms.

Before joining the University of Waterloo, I completed an M.Sc. in Applied Mathematics at ETH Zurich and a dual B.Sc. in Mathematics and Physics at Universidad de los Andes.

Research

My work lies broadly in theoretical computer science, with interests in approximation algorithms, combinatorial optimization, and graph algorithms.

  • Unsplittable multicommodity flow in special graph classes
  • Approximation algorithms for packing and assignment problems
  • Optimization under symmetric norm constraints
